# INTRODUCTION

The website must never feel like scrolling through sections.

It must feel like watching a premium product presentation.

The visitor should never think

"I'm scrolling."

Instead they should feel

"I'm discovering."

Every section is a chapter.

Every animation has meaning.

Every transition answers one question.

# GLOBAL TRANSITION RULES

Every section should naturally hand off to the next.

Avoid hard cuts.

Preferred transitions

Fade

TranslateY

Clip-path reveal

Opacity

Scale

Mask reveal

Image parallax

Background tone shifts

------------------------------------------------------------

# SCROLL STORY RULES

The user should never feel

"I reached another section."

Instead they should feel

"The story continues."

Each section must answer exactly one question before introducing the next.

------------------------------------------------------------

# MOTION TIMING

Micro-interactions

150–250ms

Cards

300–500ms

Section reveals

500–900ms

Story transitions

700–1200ms

Hero sequences

1000–1500ms

Use ease-in-out curves.

Never use abrupt easing.

------------------------------------------------------------

# CINEMATIC PRINCIPLES

Motion must be intentional.

Whitespace must create calm.

Typography must lead attention.

Images must tell stories.

Technology should feel approachable.

Premium is created through restraint, not excess.

------------------------------------------------------------

# FINAL EXPERIENCE

When visitors leave the homepage they should think:

• This company is modern.
• They clearly understand fleet technology.
• Their products feel premium.
• They look trustworthy.
• I understand what they offer.
• I want to explore more.

The homepage should feel less like a corporate website and more like a guided product experience.
